StravaBoards

Data Driven Strava Ratings

Strava, a company that adds a social and gameful aspect to tracking pace based exercises, tracks user workouts via GPS, to determine what well defined paths, or segments, they completed. The metrics that Strava collects allow them to keep an updated leaderboard for users' performance on a segment-wise basis, but currently have no notion of an overall leaderboard. The difficulty of providing this service is certainly proportional to utility it would provide, as objectively comparing users who havenâ€™t completed shared segments is mathematically challenging. My project focuses on providing the foundation for this service by demonstrating that comparing users across a range of segments is possible with the magic of linear algebra!!!





Objective

Harnessing the power of matrix decomposition the model objective rates both segments and athletes by difficulty and ability, respectively.

Unsupervised

By assuming no underlying qualities about the athletes and segments, this unsupervised model detects rider ability and segment difficulty in an intellegent way.

Useful as all Getout

With this model, athletes can now compare themselves to others outside of a single segment's scope. Someone can now be crowned the KING of STRAVA!!!

Unbiased

By looking at efforts for all segments and those subsetted into uphill and downhill the rating metrics apply to skill and difficulty within homogenous categories for accurate and useful comparisions.

Magic

Since the model is effectively a black box, I can't tell you, specifically, why any athlete is where they are on the leaderboard.

Things and Stuff

Novel way to for Strava athletes to compare their ability to others.
